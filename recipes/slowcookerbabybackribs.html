Slow Cooker Baby Back Ribs

Description

This recipe for slow cooker baby back ribs delivers tender, fall-off-the-bone meat with minimal effort. The ribs are first seasoned with a simple dry rub, a blend of spices like paprika, brown sugar, garlic powder, and onion powder, which creates a flavorful crust as they cook. Placed in the slow cooker, they braise slowly in a liquid base, often a combination of barbecue sauce, apple cider vinegar, and a touch of Worcestershire, ensuring the meat becomes incredibly moist and succulent.

After several hours of slow cooking, the ribs are removed and can be finished in the oven or on the grill for a few minutes. This final step is crucial for caramelizing the barbecue sauce that's brushed on, giving them that signature sticky, savory glaze and a slightly crisp exterior. The result is a delicious and effortless meal that captures the smoky, rich flavor of classic barbecue ribs without the need for constant monitoring, making it perfect for a relaxed family dinner or a get-together.
